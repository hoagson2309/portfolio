Volunteer Campaign

Red Flamboyant Flower Campaign (Hoa Phượng Đỏ)

A major youth volunteer campaign in Vietnam organized for high school students, young teachers, and youth union members during the summer vacation — part of the National Youth Volunteer Summer Program by the Ho Chi Minh Communist Youth Union.

History

  • First launched in Ho Chi Minh City in 1999
  • Expanded nationwide in 2015 by the Central Committee of the Ho Chi Minh Communist Youth Union
  • Today one of Vietnam's most significant youth volunteer campaigns

Objectives

  • Develop life skills and gain practical experience for students
  • Encourage volunteerism and community service among young people
  • Foster social responsibility, teamwork, and leadership skills
  • Promote civic engagement and positive contributions to society

Main Activities

  • Assisting schools in preparing for the new academic year
  • Organizing summer classes and educational programs for children
  • Environmental protection projects — tree planting and community cleanups
  • Supporting disadvantaged students and families through charitable activities
  • Visiting and assisting veterans and families with meritorious services
  • Promoting traffic safety, anti-bullying awareness, and healthy lifestyles
  • Conducting career orientation and digital skills training programs

Significance

  • Developing communication, leadership, and teamwork skills
  • Encouraging compassion, responsibility, and civic awareness
  • Providing valuable real-world experiences outside the classroom
  • Inspiring young people to become active and responsible citizens

Symbolism

Named after the red flamboyant flower (Hoa Phượng Đỏ), which blooms in summer and is closely tied to Vietnamese school life. The flower symbolizes:

Youthfulness Enthusiasm Volunteer Spirit Student Energy & Passion
